Mandu Recipe!!

Description

Korean dumplings, also known as mandu, are a traditional Korean food/street snack! These dumplings are stuffed with a mixture of meat and/or vegetables and have as many variations as there are people in Korea! This recipe is made with ground pork, onion, cabbage, tofu, mung bean noodles, and kimchi! You can prepare them in large quantities and store them in the freezer for future cooking!

Ingredients

  • Ground Pori
  • Medium Onion
  • Cabbage
  • Kimchi
  • Tofu
  • Mung bean noodles
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame Oil
  • Specific Mandu wrappers

Cooking Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bow, gently combine the ground prok, onion, cabbage, tofu, and noodles.
  2. Add the anchoby stock to water in a large, deep pan, and bring it to a boil. Once it boils, remove the stock packet and add the sauce that you made the previous step into the pot.
  3. Once the sauce is boiling, add in the rice cake and fish cakes
  4. As you let it cook, the sauce should thicken and the rice/fish cakes should become soft and chewy
  5. Next, add any optional ingredients you may want to add (eggs, cheese, noodles, etc.)
  6. Scoop into bowl and top with sesame seeds, green onions and sesame oil

Mandu Recipe!!

Description

Korean dumplings, also known as mandu, are a traditional Korean food/street snack! These dumplings are stuffed with a mixture of meat and/or vegetables and have as many variations as there are people in Korea! This recipe is made with ground pork, onion, cabbage, tofu, mung bean noodles, and kimchi! You can prepare them in large quantities and store them in the freezer for future cooking!

Ingredients

  • Ground Pori
  • Medium Onion
  • Cabbage
  • Kimchi
  • Tofu
  • Mung bean noodles
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame Oil
  • Specific Mandu wrappers

Cooking Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bow, gently combine the ground prok, onion, cabbage, tofu, and noodles.
  2. In a separate small bowl, combine the garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour seasoning mixture over meat and vegetables and mix with hands to combine.
  4. Place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of a dumpling wrapper
  5. Dip your finger in water and wet the perimeter of half of the wrapper
  6. Fold the wrapper in half, pressing to seal and then crimp the edges. Repeat until the filling is gone.
  7. You can steam, boil, fry, deep fry, or saute the dumplings as you wish!
